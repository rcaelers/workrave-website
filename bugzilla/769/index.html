Description
Jason Spiro  Aug 14 2008 01:49:13 UTC
Thank you for writing Workrave -- it's a wonderful tool.  One problem: It's too easy for new users to leave it in Quiet mode or Suspended, and to not realize why it isn't working.[1][2]

I suggest three solutions you can implement to combat this.  You can do some or all three; or you can come up with your own solutions.

1.  When you point the mouse at Workrave's tray icon, it shows a tooltip.  But the tooltip should say what mode Workrave is in.

2.  Under Preferences > User interface > General, add three option buttons (Normal, Suspended, and Quiet).  (It would be nice if you also added a one-line description of what each mode does, preferably using tooltips.)

3.  Get rid of the current quiet-mode (a sheep and door).  Instead, use a full-size dark gray sheep.  (Dark gray, not black; a black sheep would be completely invisible for the few people who have a black-colored taskbar.)

Footnotes:

^ [1].  See http://bugs.launchpad.net/ubuntu/+bug/112250 for an example of a user who didn't realize they were stuck in Quiet mode.

^ [2].  An aside:  I'm not 100% sure, but I suspect that maybe when I installed workrave-win32-1.8.5-installer.exe on my Windows XP laptop recently, it came preinstalled in Quiet mode.  This is the first time I've ever installed Workrave on this laptop, and I never imported any settings from any other computer.
